Full Body Workout

A full-body workout targets multiple muscle groups, providing a balanced approach to strength and endurance.

  1. Warm-Up (5–10 minutes)
    • Jump Rope or Jumping Jacks: 2–3 minutes to elevate heart rate.
    • Arm Circles and Shoulder Rolls: 1–2 minutes for shoulder and arm mobility.
    • Leg Swings: 1 minute per leg to activate hips and hamstrings.
  2. Squats
    • Sets and Reps: 3 sets of 12–15 reps
    • Description: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your body by bending at the knees and hips, keeping your back straight and knees over your toes. Push back up to the starting position.
    • Benefits: Works the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core, building lower body strength and stability.
  3. Push-Ups
    • Sets and Reps: 3 sets of 10–15 reps
    • Description: Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your chest to the floor by bending your elbows, then push back up to plank.
    • Benefits: Strengthens the chest, shoulders, triceps, and core, enhancing upper body endurance and stability.
  4. Bent-Over Dumbbell Rows
    • Sets and Reps: 3 sets of 10–12 reps
    • Description: Hold a dumbbell in each hand, bend forward at the hips, keeping your back flat. Pull the dumbbells toward your waist, squeezing your shoulder blades together, and then lower.
    • Benefits: Targets the upper back, lats, and biceps, improving posture and pulling strength.
  5. Lunges
    • Sets and Reps: 3 sets of 12 reps per leg
    • Description: Step forward with one leg, lowering your body until both knees are bent at 90-degree angles. Push back to the starting position and switch legs.
    • Benefits: Engages the glutes, hamstrings, quads, and core, promoting balance and lower body strength.
  6. Plank
    • Duration: 3 sets of 30–45 seconds
    • Description: Hold a plank position on your forearms and toes, keeping your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    • Benefits: Strengthens the core, shoulders, and back, enhancing overall stability and endurance.
  7. Cool Down (5–10 minutes)
    • Stretching: Focus on major muscle groups worked (quads, hamstrings, shoulders, back).
    • Breathing Exercises: Calm your breathing to promote recovery.

Cardio Workout

Cardio workouts boost cardiovascular health, burn calories, and improve endurance.

  1. Warm-Up (5 minutes)
    • Light Jog or Brisk Walk: 2–3 minutes to prepare the body for more intense activity.
    • Dynamic Stretching: Arm circles, leg swings, and hip circles.
  2. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

    HIIT involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief recovery periods, boosting heart rate and burning calories efficiently.

    • Exercise Cycle: Perform each exercise for 30 seconds, followed by 15 seconds of rest. Complete 2–3 rounds of the following exercises:
      • Burpees: Full-body movement that increases heart rate.
      • Mountain Climbers: Core and cardio workout in a plank position.
      • High Knees: Quick, high-knee steps to engage the lower body.
      • Jump Squats: Explosive squats to work the legs and glutes.
  3. Steady-State Cardio (15–20 minutes)
    • Options: Choose an activity like running, cycling, or rowing at a steady, moderate intensity.
    • Pace: Aim for a pace that keeps your heart rate at a moderate level (approximately 60–70% of max heart rate).
    • Benefits: Improves endurance, cardiovascular health, and calorie burn over a sustained period.
  4. Cool Down (5–10 minutes)
    • Walking or Light Jogging: Gradually slow down your pace to bring your heart rate down.
    • Stretching: Focus on the lower body and core muscles used during the workout (quads, hamstrings, calves, and hips).
